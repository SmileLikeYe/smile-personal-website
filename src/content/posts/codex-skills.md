I like skills because they are more concrete than prompts. A good skill captures a situation, the steps that matter, the failure modes, and the verification loop.

For my own work, skills are useful in three places:

1. Product debugging: start from the real current state, not memory.
2. Delivery: build, test, review, commit, and keep unrelated files out.
3. Frontend polish: compare screenshots, inspect layout, and fix visual drift.

The best skills are boring in the right way. They remove the repeated decision cost, so the next run can focus on judgment.

That is also how I want this site to work: the public surface shows not only projects, but the operating system behind the projects.
